Networking Playbook

Finding the Right People

Target Hiring Managers & Decision Makers

  • Use LinkedIn's advanced search filters to find people by company, title, and location
  • Look for "Hiring Manager", "VP", "Director", "Head of" titles in your target departments
  • Check company pages for recent hires and team announcements
  • Use mutual connections to find warm introductions

Research Strategy

  • Read their recent posts and comments to understand their interests
  • Check their company's recent news and initiatives
  • Look for shared experiences (alumni, previous companies, interests)
  • Note their communication style and tone

Initial Outreach

Message Structure

  1. Personal Connection: Reference something specific about them or their work
  2. Your Value: Briefly mention your background and what you bring
  3. Clear Intent: State why you're reaching out (learn, network, explore opportunities)
  4. Soft Ask: Suggest a low-commitment next step (coffee chat, brief call)

Best Practices

  • Keep messages under 200 words
  • Be specific and personal, not generic
  • Show genuine interest in their work/company
  • Don't ask for a job directly in the first message
  • Include a clear call-to-action

Timing

  • Send messages Tuesday-Thursday, 10 AM - 2 PM
  • Avoid Monday mornings and Friday afternoons
  • Follow up after 1-2 weeks if no response

Building Relationships

Relationship Building Timeline

  • Week 1-2: Initial connection and first conversation
  • Week 3-4: Follow up with value (share relevant article, congratulate on achievement)
  • Month 2-3: Regular check-ins, offer help or insights
  • Month 3+: Deeper relationship, potential for referrals

Value-Add Strategies

  • Share relevant industry articles or insights
  • Congratulate them on achievements or milestones
  • Offer to help with their projects or challenges
  • Introduce them to other valuable connections
  • Provide feedback on their content or initiatives

Asking for Opportunities

When to Ask

  • After 2-3 meaningful interactions
  • When you've provided value to them
  • When they've shown interest in your background
  • During natural conversation flow

How to Ask

  • Be specific about what you're looking for
  • Frame it as seeking advice or guidance
  • Mention you're exploring opportunities in their field/company
  • Ask if they know of any openings or can refer you to the right person
  • Always offer to return the favor
